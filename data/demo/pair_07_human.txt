I bought an EV two years ago mostly out of curiosity. My partner thought I was being impulsive — we'd had our old hatchback for nine years and it still ran fine. But I'd been reading obsessively about range anxiety (overblown, as it turned out) and charging infrastructure (still genuinely patchy), so eventually I just decided to live the experiment.

Here's the honest report: I love it for everything within my city and the surrounding region. Plugging in overnight is so much simpler than I expected. The torque is addictive. I've saved a real amount on fuel.

But road trips require planning in a way gas cars don't. I've sat at a broken fast charger in the middle of nowhere, sweating, recalculating routes. I've made peace with that. My partner has not entirely made peace with that.

The bigger picture stuff matters more to me now that I'm actually in it. EVs are only as clean as their grid and their battery supply chain — and those are both still pretty messy. It's better than combustion, I believe that. But the "zero emissions" branding bothers me. We've just moved the emissions elsewhere, and in some places upstream. I'm not saying don't buy one. I'm saying go in with eyes open.
