I grew up in a house with terrible insulation and a gas furnace that my dad babied like a second child. Every winter, the heating bill was this looming dread — he'd walk around turning off lights in rooms nobody was in, lecturing us about phantom loads. I thought he was being absurd. Then I started paying my own utilities and understood completely.

So when I hear people argue that renewable energy is too expensive, I genuinely don't know what they're comparing it to. Solar costs have fallen something like ninety percent over the past decade. Wind is often the cheapest electricity in history right now, full stop. The "too expensive" argument is simply outdated, and I wish more politicians knew that.

What's still hard is storage and grid flexibility. The sun doesn't shine at night, wind is intermittent — anyone who's read even a little about energy knows this. But battery storage is improving fast, and grid interconnection across regions can smooth out a lot of variability. We're not waiting for a miracle. We're waiting for policy.

My dad eventually got solar panels on that house. He sends me his monthly power bill sometimes. It's so low it's almost a joke. He still turns off lights when he leaves rooms, though. Some habits don't die.
