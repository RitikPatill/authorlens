I bought Bitcoin in 2017 because my college friend wouldn't stop texting me about it. I put in a thousand dollars, watched it roughly triple, then watched it crater, then forgot about it for three years because I couldn't bear to look. When I finally checked in 2021, I was briefly very happy. Then 2022 happened.

Here's what I've come to think: crypto is simultaneously a genuinely novel financial technology and one of the largest speculative bubbles in modern history, and both of those things can be true at once. The blockchain-as-infrastructure argument has real merit. The "this will replace fiat currency" argument seems increasingly unlikely to me.

What bothers me most is the culture. The relentless hype, the shame toward skeptics, the "have fun staying poor" energy that permeated every forum for years. Legitimate technologies don't usually need that kind of psychological coercion.

I kept a small position, mostly as a reminder that I know nothing about timing markets. It's gone up, come down, gone up again. I've stopped attaching feelings to the number. The honest lesson from my crypto experience is that I was doing FOMO-investing, not thinking — and that's a trap that has nothing to do with the underlying asset and everything to do with how I handle uncertainty.
