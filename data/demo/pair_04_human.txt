I quit Instagram for three months last year and it was one of the strangest, most clarifying experiments I've ever done. The first week was genuinely uncomfortable — my hand kept reaching for my phone at odd moments, like a phantom limb. I hadn't realized how deep the reflex had gotten.

By week three, though, something shifted. I started noticing things more. Conversations felt longer. Books went down faster. I wasn't constantly half-present, my brain churning through whatever I'd seen five minutes ago. It was like turning off a low-grade noise I'd stopped hearing.

The thing nobody tells you about social media is that it's not just time it takes — it's attention bandwidth. I'd open the app for thirty seconds and then spend the next twenty minutes with a weird, dissatisfied feeling I couldn't name. The comparison engine runs whether you want it to or not.

I'm back on now, but differently. I muted ninety percent of accounts, I don't have it on my phone's home screen, and I check it on purpose rather than reflexively. Does it still drain me sometimes? Yeah. But I feel like I understand what I'm trading now. Before, I didn't even know I was trading anything.
